A Taste of Our Curricula

Course #1: Starting Your Own Company
Coming Spring 2017

  • Chapter 01


    Finding Your Idea

    How should you spend your time? Who should you surround yourself with? What are your strengths? What are your passions? What could be improved in your community? Is there a market? Are people willing to pay?

  • Chapter 02


    Seeking Feedback and Having Humility

    Who should you talk to? How should you share your ideas with them? How do you run effective customer interviews? What questions should you ask? Why is humility important? How do you document your findings? How are you going to prototype rapidly?

  • Chapter 03


    Research

    How do you conduct research? Why is research important? How do you learn about the market size/opportunity/competitive environment? How do you conduct interviews? What is the level of messaging and design that your customer connects with? How can you figure out how many people actually NEED your product and are willing to pay for it?

  • Chapter 04


    Networking

    What does networking actually mean? Why is it so important? How do you start to build a strong network? How do you leverage your network for success? How do you maintain a strong and meaningful network?

  • Chapter 05


    Co-Founders

    Why do you need a co-founder? What should you consider when working with friends? How do you determine complementary traits between founders? How do you have difficult conversations? How do you assign roles and responsibilities?

  • Chapter 06


    Branding and Marketing

    What does this mean? What is the difference between branding, marketing and advertising? Why is high-quality content important? How do you create a strong brand? What are some free resources for creating content? How and why should you create a target-customer persona? How do you change your narrative to fit your audience? How do you understand your consumer? How do you stay true to your values as a founder?

  • Chapter 07


    Production and Pricing

    How are you going to actually create your product? How do you manage manufacturing costs? What resources can you use to manufacture an early product? How do you test your product with users to find the perfect price? How do you ask the right questions? How do you decide on a financial model? How do you figure out your cost of services? What are the basic forms of funding and why would you need them?

  • Chapter 08


    Selling

    How do you take your concept to market? How and why do you document your growth and build a portfolio? How do you leverage your portfolio to lead to more sales? How do you learn more about your customer, such as where they spend their time and make purchase decisions? What avenues are most effective to sell through?

  • Chapter 09


    Building a Team

    How do you identify your needs for growth? How do you attract and hire the right people to fill the gaps? How do you create a "culture" that can spread throughout the organization? What are some aspects of strong leadership? How do you get the most out of your team?

  • Chapter 10


    Overcoming Failure

    How do you identify when things aren't working? How do you get through it? How do you prepare yourself on a regular basis for intended failure? How do you shift your perspective to view failure as a positive thing? How do you "fail fast and fail forward"?
